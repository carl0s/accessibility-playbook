# [Giving a Damn] Ch 1: Technology doesn't make accessibility hard — people who don't give a damn do

## Core Idea
Every accessibility difficulty traces to one root cause: people not giving a damn about others who are different. Technology and standards have existed for 20+ years; the blocker is human resistance, and it is an ethics problem, not a technical one.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Resistance Archetypes** — name the person blocking accessibility so you can counter them:
  1. **Allergic to change** ("we've always done it this way"; "NIH syndrome" — Not Invented Here). Counter: accessibility is PFE — *Proudly Found Elsewhere* (W3C made WCAG).
  2. **Wants "the business case."** Counter: ADA/ACA/DDA are *civil rights* laws; demanding a business case to stop discriminating is **ableist**.
  3. **Wants detailed proof for every recommendation.** Counter: WCAG provides *some* proof; remember WCAG is non-prescriptive ("a mechanism," not "a button").
  4. **Prioritizes new inaccessible features over fixing old ones.** (Twitter Voice Notes; Dead by Daylight colorblind fiasco.)
  5. **"It only impacts a small number of users."** Counter: 26% of US population has a disability; making the harmed group seem tiny is an "ethics-violation emotional compensation strategy."
  6. **"Disabled people aren't our target audience."** Counter: circular fallacy — they can't be customers if the product excludes them; disability is the largest US minority.

## Key Concepts
- **Ableism** — beliefs/practices that devalue and discriminate against people with disabilities; rests on the assumption disabled people need to be "fixed."
- **The ableism substitution test** — swap "disability" for another underrepresented group; if the statement becomes obviously unacceptable ("software People of Color can't use"), it's ableist.
- **Accessibility debt** — the compounding cost/risk of staying inaccessible (3,550 US digital lawsuits filed in 2020).
- **Accessibility overlays/plugins/widgets** — "one line of code fixes everything" vendors; claims rated "pants on fire"; ~10% of late-2020 lawsuits were *against* overlay users.

## Mental Models
- **"Demanding a business case for accessibility is ableist."** Civil rights don't need an ROI.
- **The substitution test** — fastest way to expose an ableist attitude.
- **Overlays are a trap, not a fix** — and may contractually block you from recovering litigation losses.
- **Inaccessible products are broken products.** The first step to fix them is to give a damn.

## Anti-patterns
- **NIH syndrome** — refusing W3C/WCAG because it wasn't invented here.
- **Waiting for a lawsuit/lost customer** before valuing accessibility (the savings have "zero value" to this mindset).
- **Buying an accessibility overlay** and believing it makes you litigation-proof.
- **Minimizing the affected population** to excuse shipping an inaccessible feature.

## Key Takeaways
1. The root cause of inaccessibility is people, not technology.
2. It takes ~4 hours to start building accessibly; ~1 week to be competent in several areas.
3. Solve it: make the investment + embrace change; reward accessible releases (Apple ties bonuses to it); hire people with disabilities; secure vocal executive support.
4. Use the substitution test to call out ableism.
5. Avoid overlays/widgets — they invite lawsuits, not protect from them.
