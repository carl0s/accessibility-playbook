# Play 7: Be part of an accessible culture

## Core Idea
Standards and strategy fail if the organization's culture doesn't support accessibility — "culture eats strategy for breakfast." Because no org can write a procedure for every situation, staff will adapt based on culture; so culture, repeated messaging, and governance are what make accessibility durable.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Role-based responsibilities** — everyone has a part:
  - **Employee → Be an ally.** Question status quo when policies/practices undermine accessibility; suggest new ways.
  - **Team Leader/Manager → Be an agent of change.** Promote awareness, invite guest speakers, build accessibility into performance agreements, allow staff extra time to serve.
  - **Executive → Lead by example.** Put accessibility at the forefront of decisions; ensure every design decision considered it; tie it to efficiency/performance messaging.

- **Three steps to move culture toward accessibility**:
  1. **Express your values** — when values and culture align, they move the org forward (Values & Ethics Code: "Respect for People"; Canadian Human Rights Act; Accessible Canada Act).
  2. **Embed accessibility in governance** — it isn't a one-time question; bake it into investment approval, exception decisions, progress tracking, and clear accountability.
  3. **Awareness & understanding** — raising collective awareness (training, info, regular discussion) is what actually changes culture, beyond specialist training.

- **Governance questions to ask**: Is there a clear understanding of the standards? Do exceptions require an explicit decision? Is accessibility mandatory for investment approval? Is progress tracked/reported? Is it clear who is accountable?

## Key Concepts
- **Culture** — the attitudes and behaviours that determine what's encouraged or discouraged; reflected in priorities, resource allocation, and daily treatment of persons with disabilities.
- **Legacy policies** — older rules/procedures that re-enforce an outdated culture and create hurdles for staff trying to help.
- **Repeated messaging** — shapes attitudes/behaviours, aligns thinking and doing, defines what's accepted, resonates when shared often.

## Mental Models
- **"Culture eats strategy for breakfast."** Ingrained attitudes will beat an accessibility strategy unless culture changes too.
- **Culture counters good intentions over time** — it lives in beliefs and behaviours, so it must be actively, repeatedly shaped.
- **A renewed focus on accessibility must be holistic** — not bolted onto an outdated policy base.

## Anti-patterns
- **Treating accessibility as a one-time compliance checkbox** rather than an ongoing governance requirement.
- **Letting legacy policies stand** that quietly signal accessibility isn't a priority.
- **Talking about accessibility only when convenient / when budgets allow** — culture forms from what's repeated.

## Key Takeaways
1. Everyone has a role: ally (employee), change agent (manager), example-setter (executive).
2. Align stated values with daily behaviour to move the organization.
3. Embed accessibility in governance: investment gates, exception decisions, tracking, accountability.
4. Raise collective awareness continuously — that's what shifts culture.
5. Audit legacy policies that re-enforce an outdated culture.
