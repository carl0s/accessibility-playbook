# Inspiring Tools: templates, checklists & assessment

## Core Idea
The playbook ships four reusable instruments to operationalize the seven plays: a CX measurement model, a business-intelligence model, journey-map templates, and a self-scoring accessibility assessment checklist.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **CX Survey Measurement Model** — Service Canada's consistent framework for assessing client service experience across each stage of the client journey (implemented 2017–18). Synthesized from the **Common Measurements Tool (CMT)** (ICCS), Quebec's client survey model, and Forrester's CX approach.

- **Accessible Client Service Journey Map — two templates**:
  - **Current State**: across journey phases **Aware → Inform → Advise → Identify → Apply → Manage**, map for each persona their Client Actions, Client Interactions (channel used/preferred), Service Barriers ("what prevents the client from…"), and Client Experience (emotions/feelings).
  - **Future State**: across channels (Online / Phone / In-Person), map the persona's reality, **Service Improvements** (assistive tech + policy/service design + employee training that reduce barriers per channel), and the resulting Client Experience.

- **Accessible Client Service Assessment Checklist** — a Yes/No self-audit. "Yes" answers list existing good practices; "No" answers become a backlog of improvements. Sections:
  - **In-person**: physical environment, emergencies, waiting area, service transaction, computer workstations.
  - **Phone**: TTY, automated call-backs, "press 0", IVR.
  - **Online**: WCAG 2.1 AA, plain language, AT compatibility, zoom, captions, no-time-limit forms, progress bars.
  - **Feedback & engagement**: cross-channel feedback, co-design/UX testing with persons with disabilities.
  - **Policy design**: agents can take needed time, fill forms on behalf, authorize delegated third-party representatives.
  - **Communications**: multi-channel, grade-8 reading level, inclusive images, accessible formats, signage at universal eye level.
  - **Training**: clear language, recognize VRS delay, one piece of info at a time, recognize fatigue, reduce noise.
  - **Culture**: accessibility criteria for funding, data-driven decisions, international best practices, mandatory training, dedicated front-line training.

## Key Concepts
- **Current vs Future State journey map** — diagnose barriers now, then design the improved channel experience.
- **Self-scoring audit** — the gap between Yes and No *is* the roadmap.
- **Business Intelligence Model** — referenced framework for bringing client data together to support decisions (see Ch 3).

## Mental Models
- **Map the journey, not just the transaction** — barriers live across Aware→Manage, not only at "Apply".
- **The "No" column is your project plan.**

## Key Takeaways
1. Use Current-State maps to find barriers per persona per phase; Future-State maps to design fixes per channel.
2. Run the assessment checklist; treat every "No" as a candidate improvement.
3. Track CX with a consistent model across all channels.
4. Policy-design answers (act on behalf, take needed time, delegate authority) are often the highest-leverage fixes.
